Slow correspondence is often more generative than fast-paced or synchronous conversation

Question brought up by Stian Håklev (1)

Slow long-lived iterative discussions/brainstorm that accrete... interlinking digital gardens, blog rings, postal chess?

In our times of unprecedented speed of communication, it can be worth taking the time to slow down and mull over responses. Many of the great scientists in history maintained correspondences by sending letters to each other—today, this slow form of communication is inconcievable to many people, but in fact it can result in much more generative and productive conversations.

In the context of education,

I like to think of Writing as Distilled Thought, the process of writing can help to condense and organize your thoughts around a topic.
